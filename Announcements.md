                                        <h3>Mini Puzzle: Vegas Baby</h3>
                                        <div dir="auto">True story.<br><br>When I go to Vegas I always play roulette. &nbsp;I utilize the following strategy: I start with $255. I bet&nbsp;$1 on black (or any other even money bet). &nbsp;If I lose, I double my bet. &nbsp;Each time I lose, I double the previous bet. &nbsp;When I win, I reset back to $1.<br><br>I continue this strategy until my winnings total $70 (or I run out of money).<br><br>Question: What is the probability I will head home a winner with $70?</div>

                                        <h3>Mini Puzzle: Enumeration Cogitation</h3>
                                        <div dir="auto">Here's a short one that's relevant to some of the work we're doing in class now...<br><br>Given a list of stock symbols, write a program, enumerate.py that will enumerate and print every portfolio of 4 stocks drawn from that list. &nbsp;Note that order doesn't matter, so<br><br>&nbsp; &nbsp; SPY QQQ GLD RSP&nbsp;is equivalent to&nbsp;QQQ SPY GLD RSP<br><div><br>And you should only print one of them.<br><br></div><div>The program that accomplishes this in the fewest number of characters as determined by the unix command "wc -m enumerate.py" is the winner.&nbsp;</div><div><br>Here's the list of symbols:&nbsp;['SPY','IBM','GLD','AAA','RSP','QQQ']<br><br>Here's the output of my solution. 220 characters:<br><br><p style="margin-bottom: 0px; font-size: 12px; line-height: normal; font-family: Courier;">$ python enumerate.py</p>
